# DiscoWorld: Electronic Music History Atlas

## 1. ORIGINS (1950s-1970s)

**Paris** — Pierre Schaeffer's musique concrète (1948): tape loops, found sounds. Academic, cold, experimental. Karlheinz Stockhausen in Cologne pushed further with pure electronic synthesis.
- **3D Visual:** Brutalist laboratory, reel-to-reel machines.

**Düsseldorf** — Kraftwerk (1970-): Kling Klang studio. Man-machine aesthetic, motorik rhythm, autobahn imagery. Spawned the entire DNA of electronic pop.
- **3D Visual:** Clean geometric highways, neon grids, robotic mannequins.

**Munich** — Giorgio Moroder: "I Feel Love" (1977) = first fully synthesized disco track. Four-on-the-floor + Moog sequencer. Italo disco followed.
- **3D Visual:** Chrome, mirrorball, Mediterranean nightlife glow.

**Kingston** — King Tubby, Lee Scratch Perry: dub mixing desk as instrument. Echo, delay, space. Direct ancestor of bass music, dubstep, all remix culture.
- **3D Visual:** Corrugated iron studios, speaker stacks, tropical decay.

**Berlin/Cologne** — Tangerine Dream, Klaus Schulze, Can: kosmische musik. Long-form, modular synths, cosmic.
- **3D Visual:** Dark nebulae, pulsing analog waveforms.

**Tech:** Moog Modular, Buchla, Mellotron, tape machines, spring reverbs, mixing desk-as-instrument (dub).

## 2. THE BIRTH (1980-1988)

**Chicago** — Frankie Knuckles at The Warehouse (1977-83), Ron Hardy at Music Box. Disco edits + drum machines = house. Marshall Jefferson "Move Your Body," Phuture "Acid Tracks" (TB-303 accident). Trax Records, DJ International. Black, gay, working-class.
- **3D Visual:** Warehouse interiors, sweat-dripping walls, red-lit dancefloors, South Side industrial.

**Detroit** — The Belleville Three: Juan Atkins (Cybotron, Metroplex), Derrick May (Transmat), Kevin Saunderson (KMS). Post-industrial Motown collapse as backdrop. Techno = Kraftwerk + Parliament + emptied factories.
- **3D Visual:** Abandoned auto plants, blue-steel cold, geometric minimalism, highway overpasses.

**New York** — Larry Levan at Paradise Garage (1977-87): the bridge. Disco's death (1979 Comiskey Park) pushed it underground. Loft (David Mancuso), Sound Factory (Junior Vasquez).
- **3D Visual:** Loft spaces, disco-to-house gradient, Manhattan skyline at 4am.

**Sheffield** — Cabaret Voltaire, Human League, Heaven 17: industrial meets pop synth.
**Manchester** — FAC 51 Hacienda (1982): Factory Records + New Order bankrolled it. Proto-rave before rave.
- **3D Visual:** Northern English brick, rain, neon signage.

**Belgium** — Front 242, Nitzer Ebb: EBM (Electronic Body Music). Then New Beat (1987): slowed-down EBM in Boccaccio club, Ghent.
- **3D Visual:** Low-ceiling Belgian clubs, strobe-heavy.

**Branch points:** Disco → House (Chicago, 1983-84). Electro → Techno (Detroit, 1985). Post-punk → EBM (Belgium/Sheffield, 1981).

**Tech:** TR-808, TR-909, TB-303, Juno-106, SH-101, SP-1200, reel-to-reel edits, 4-track portastudios.

---

## 3. THE EXPLOSION (1988-1995)

**London/South East England** — Second Summer of Love (1988): Shoom, Spectrum, orbital motorway raves. MDMA arrival. Criminal Justice Act 1994. Jungle/DnB split: Metalheadz (Goldie), Moving Shadow, Reinforced. Amen break + ragga vocals + 160+ BPM.
- **3D Visual:** M25 orbital fields, laser rigs in warehouses, neon smiley faces → dark urban concrete for jungle.

**Berlin** — Wall falls Nov 1989. Empty East Berlin buildings = illegal parties. Tresor opens 1991 in Wertheim vault basement. E-Werk, Bunker. Hard Wax = Detroit→Berlin import pipeline. Basic Channel (von Oswald, Ernestus) = dub techno born.
- **3D Visual:** Crumbling GDR architecture, vaulted basements, concrete dust, strobe-only lighting.

**Frankfurt** — Sven Väth, Dorian Gray club (airport!), Harthouse, Eye Q Records: trance origin. Spread to Goa via hippie trail.
- **3D Visual:** Airport terminal rave, psychedelic fractals.

**Rotterdam** — Gabber/hardcore: Thunderdome. 160-200 BPM, distorted kicks. Working-class, aggressive.
- **3D Visual:** Sports hall raves, industrial port.

**Bristol** — Massive Attack "Blue Lines" (1991), Portishead, Tricky: trip-hop. Wild Bunch sound system culture.
- **3D Visual:** Foggy harbour, Victorian architecture, slow smoke, blue-grey palette.

**Warp Records (Sheffield)** — Artificial Intelligence series (1992): IDM born. Aphex Twin, Autechre, Black Dog.
- **3D Visual:** Abstract digital landscapes, glitch patterns.

**Branch points:** House → Acid house (1987). Breakbeat hardcore → Jungle (1992) → DnB (1994). Trance splits from techno (1991). Ambient splits from rave (chill-out rooms → standalone genre).

## 4. THE DIVERSIFICATION (1995-2005)

**Berlin** — Love Parade peaks (1999: 1.5M people). Minimal techno: Robert Hood, Richie Hawtin. Panorama Bar opens.
**Cologne** — Kompakt: Wolfgang Voigt, Michael Mayer. Microhouse: tiny clicks, glitch-pop, warm.
- **3D Visual:** Berlin = raw concrete monoliths; Cologne = pristine, warm minimalism.

**London** — UK garage → 2-step → grime (Wiley, Dizzee Rascal, Bow E3). FWD>> at Plastic People (2001) = dubstep seeds. Croydon = dubstep's Belleville.
- **3D Visual:** South London council estates, bass vibrating car windows, pirate radio towers.

**Paris** — French touch: Daft Punk "Homework" (1997), Cassius, AIR. Filtered disco loops.
- **3D Visual:** Parisian apartment studios, retro-futurist, chrome robots.

**Ibiza** — Superclub era: Pacha, Amnesia, Space. Progressive house/trance peaks commercially.
- **3D Visual:** White island, open-air terraces, sunrise sets.

**Tech:** Ableton Live (2001). CDJs replace vinyl. Reaktor, Reason, Fruity Loops democratize production.

---

## 5. THE DIGITAL ERA (2005-2015)

**Las Vegas/US festivals** — EDM explosion: Deadmau5, Skrillex, Swedish House Mafia. Ultra, EDC. Dubstep mutates to American mid-range aggression.
- **3D Visual:** LED-saturated mega-stages, confetti cannons, corporate neon.

**Berlin** — Berghain (opens 2004): global techno temple. Dettmann, Klock, Function.
- **3D Visual:** Heizkraftwerk (power plant), brutalist cathedral, perpetual night.

**Global/Internet** — SoundCloud, Bandcamp, RA = media decentralization. Lo-fi house, vaporwave.
- **3D Visual:** Pixelated, retro-digital, bedroom studio aesthetic.

---

## 6. THE PRESENT (2015-2026)

**Global/fragmented** — Deconstructed club: Arca, SOPHIE, Jlin. Hard techno revival: SPFDJ, 999999999. Breakbeat nostalgia: Overmono, Skee Mask.
- **3D Visual:** Chrome-liquid surfaces, dystopian-queer aesthetics.

**Johannesburg/Lagos/London** — Amapiano: log-drum bass, South African township origin → global.
- **3D Visual:** Vibrant colour, open-air, township textures, sunlight.

**Internet-native** — Hyperpop fragments. AI tools. Post-COVID outdoor raves.

**Tech:** Eurorack modular revival, AI composition, spatial audio.
